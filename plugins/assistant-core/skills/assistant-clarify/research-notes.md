# Research Notes

This skill is based on a small set of durable communication and counseling findings that transfer well to AI clarification work.

## Principles used

1. **Common ground before action**
   Successful communication depends on shared assumptions about what both sides know and mean. When prompts are compressed or implicit, first establish a shared frame instead of acting on a private interpretation.

2. **Reflective listening**
   Paraphrasing the user's likely meaning helps reveal intent and lowers the odds of defensive repair cycles. Reflection should come before dense questioning.

3. **Open questions plus summaries**
   Open questions invite the missing context, while summaries verify that the conversation is converging rather than drifting.

4. **Structured, collaborative interviewing**
   Structured interviews outperform loose probing when cognition is noisy or overloaded. A short, well-bounded Q&A is easier to answer than broad requests for clarification.

## Translation into agent behavior

- Reflect the likely goal in plain language before executing
- Ask no more than 1-3 high-yield questions
- Prefer multiple-choice or recommended defaults when possible
- End the clarification loop with a confirmed structured brief
